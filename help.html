A viewer-run control deck for Quin69's AI Slop Survivors. This page explains exactly how it works, what it can and can't do, and how to reach us.

🔒 Is this safe? How your account is protected

Short version: you log in on Twitch's own site, this app never sees your password, and the only thing it can ever do with your account is send a short list of game commands to chat — which you can revoke in one click.

You never give us your password

Login uses Twitch's official OAuth (Authorization Code) flow. You're sent to Twitch's real login page; you type your password there, on Twitch's servers. This app never sees, receives, or stores your Twitch password — ever.

No secret ever touches your browser

OAuth apps have a "client secret." Ours lives only on a small server-side function (a Cloudflare Worker) — never in this web page, never in the code you download, never on your device. The browser does the login handshake; the server holds the secret and does the token exchange privately.

No login token is ever put in the URL

Because we use the Authorization Code flow, the only thing that briefly appears in your address bar after login is a single-use code that is useless to anyone without the server-side secret (and it expires in seconds). Your actual access token never appears in the URL — safe to use even on stream.

What we store — almost nothing

  • Your Twitch access token is kept in your own browser's local storage so you stay logged in. It never leaves your device except to talk to Twitch.
  • There is no database, no server-side account, no analytics, and no tracking. We don't collect your email, name, or any personal data.
  • Log out (or clear your browser data) and the token is gone.

It can only send a fixed list of commands

This app is hard-wired to send only the specific game commands listed below. It cannot type arbitrary messages, cannot read your chat or DMs, and cannot act as you anywhere else.

Open source — verify it yourself

The entire site is public. Read every line here: github.com/Yhipc/AI-Slop-Survivors-Controller.

Revoke access anytime

Go to Twitch → Settings → Connections, find this app, and click Disconnect. That instantly invalidates its access.

🪪 What Twitch permission it asks for

When you log in, Twitch shows you a consent screen. This app requests exactly one permission (scope):

user:write:chat — “Send chat messages to a channel on your behalf.”

It also reads your basic public identity (your user ID and display name) so it knows who is sending — this needs no special permission beyond being logged in.

✔ What it CAN do

  • Send the game commands below to Quin69's chat, as you, when you click a button.
  • See your public username / user ID.

✘ What it CANNOT do

  • See your password or email.
  • Read your chat history, whispers, or DMs.
  • Change account settings, follow/unfollow, moderate, or spend anything.
  • Post anywhere else, or send any message other than the fixed commands.

🎮 Every command this app can send

These are the only messages this app is capable of sending to Twitch chat. What each one does in-game is decided by AI Slop Survivors, not by this app.

!joinJoin the game
!spawnSpawn / summon
!fleeFlee
!aoeArea-of-effect
!dmgDeal damage
!hpRestore health
!speedSpeed up
!boostBoost
!explodeExplode
!invulnerabilityInvulnerable
!fartFart cloud
!evolveKevinEvolve: Kevin
!thornsThorns
!evolvesuccubusEvolve: Succubus
!succSucc
!evolvewoodlandjoeEvolve: Woodland Joe

Note: each tile has a 30-second cooldown after use, and Twitch enforces its own per-user rate limits and channel rules (slow mode, followers-only, etc.), which may drop a message independently of this app.
